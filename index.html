The Tragedy of 1926

In the dark era of the Japanese occupation, two brilliant souls met in Tokyo. Yun Sim-deok, Korea's first professional soprano, and Kim U-jin, a genius playwright constrained by duty.

Their love was a rebellion against a world that tried to silence them. Unable to find freedom in life, they sought it in death. On August 4, 1926, aboard a ferry crossing the Korea Strait, they vanished into the dark waters together, leaving behind only their art.

"I am leaving this world, where money is everything, to go to a place where love is everything."
